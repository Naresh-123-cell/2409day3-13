KRISHNA JANMASHTAMI FESTIVAL

The Krishna Janmashtami festival marks the birth of Krishna, one of the most popular deities worshipped by Hindus. He is thought to have been born in 3228 BC. His birthday is celebrated eight days after Raksha Bandhan, a festival celebrating the bond between brothers and sisters.

celebrate Krishna Janmashtami:

Fasting

Devotees observe a strict fast throughout the day, abstaining from grains and certain foods, and breaking it only after midnight.

Midnight vigil

Devotees keep vigil through the night, known as Jagran, to welcome the divine child into their lives

Dahi Handi

A pot of sweetened butter and curds is suspended in midair, and teenage boys dressed as cowherds climb on each other's backs to break the pot and share the sweet yogurt

Respect all living beings

Treat all creatures with kindness, especially on Janmashtami.

Ratha Yatra

In Odisha, the Jagannath Temple in Puri performs a Ratha Yatra during Janmashtami.
