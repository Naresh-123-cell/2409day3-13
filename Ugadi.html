UGADI FESTIVAL

Ugadi is celebrated on the first day of the Hindu month of Chaitra (Marchâ€“April) following the South Indian Amavasyant calendar, in which months end on the day of the new moon. The new year marks the beginning of the calendar cycle as well as the start of spring and the planting season. The correspondence of the holiday with the waxing moon in the first month of the Hindu year symbolizes growth.
Ugadi is a joyful holiday, and celebrants begin the day by bathing, applying oil to the body, putting on new clothes, and offering puja. Hindu women also draw intricate geometric designs, called rangoli, in front of the home. Also important is attending a temple to hear a recitation of the astrological almanac for the coming year

Ugadi Pachadi

The 6 ingredients with different flavors that are added in the Ugadi Pachadi, further linked to the 6 human emotions are:

1.Tamarind

Tamarind has a sour taste.Tamarind is sour when young, but becomes sweeter and more sour as it ripens. Its flavor is often compared to a balance of tangy lemon

2.Jaggery

Jaggery has a sweet taste.Jaggery is made of the products of sugarcane and the toddy palm tree. The sugar made from the sap of the date palm is more prized and less commonly available outside of the regions where it is made. The toddy palm is tapped for producing jaggery in India, Bangladesh, Pakistan, Nepal, Myanmar and Sri Lanka.

3.Unripe or raw mango

Unripe has a tangy, astringent taste.Raw mangoes may be preferable due to their higher vitamin C content. For antioxidant intake: Ripe mangoes may be more beneficial due to their increased levels of antioxidants like beta-carotene.

4.Neem flowers

Neem flowers has a bitter taste.Neem flower is the blossom of the neem tree, a tropical evergreen native to India, Pakistan, and Bangladesh. The neem flower has a bitter taste and is commonly used in traditional medicine to help with a variety of ailments.

5.Black pepper

Black pepper has a spicy, hot taste.Pepper goes by many names in different languages such as hu jiao (Chinese), kali mirch (Hindi), pimienta (Spanish), fulful / filfil (Arabic), poivre (French), pepe (Italian), and pfeffer

6.Salt

Salt has a salty taste.Salt is a mineral composed primarily of sodium chloride (NaCl). When used in food, especially in granulated form, it is more formally called table salt.
