DUSSEHRA FESTIVAL

Dussehra, also known as Vijayadashami, is a major Hindu festival that celebrates the triumph of good over evil. It's celebrated on the tenth day of Ashvina, the seventh month of the Hindu calendar, and usually falls in September or October.

Here are some ways Dussehra is celebrated:

Processions: People carry clay statues of deities like Durga, Lakshmi, and Ganesha to a river or ocean, where they are immersed in water.
Ram Leela: A theatrical version of the Ramayana is performed in Delhi.
Doll shows: In Tamil Nadu, families arrange doll shows.
Pata jatra: A unique ritual in Chhattisgarh that involves worshiping wood.
Mysore Dasara: An exhibition with stalls selling clothes, kitchenware, and more, as well as a play area with a Ferris wheel.
Family gatherings: Families come together to pray, share sweets, and bake with each other.
Fireworks: People watch huge fires and enjoy colorful fireworks in the night sky.

Dussehra term is related to the killing of the 10-headed demon Ravana and so Dussehra literally means removal as well take away 10 sins. It is believed that Dussehra rituals are intended to get rid of the 10 human weaknesses and also bad qualities from the person.

Symbolizing the victory of good over evil, Dussehra is celebrated on the 10th day of the month of Ashvina (September–October), the seventh month of the Hindu calendar, with the appearance of the full moon, an event called the “bright fortnight”
