PONGAL FESTIVAL

Pongal is a multi-day Hindu harvest festival celebrated by Tamils. It is observed in the month of Thai according to the Tamil solar calendar and usually falls on 14 or 15 January and hence is also referred to as Thai Pongal.

pongal is celebrated for three days

1.Bhogi Pongal

The first day of the festival, which is a day to spend with family. People clean their homes, light bonfires to burn away negativity, and make a special Pongal meal.
"Bhogi" comes from the Sanskrit word "Bhog," which denotes pleasure and gluttony. The main purpose of Bhogi Pongal is to honour Lord Indra, the god of rain, and it represents the discarding of useless items to make room for a new beginning and crop.

2.Surya Pongal

Surya Pongal is the second day of the Pongal festival, a multi-day harvest festival that honors the Sun God, Surya, and marks the start of spring
The second day of the Pongal festival is Surya Pongal, which is the main day of celebration.People draw colorful floor patterns called kolam at the entrance of their homes.Households cook a pot of fresh rice with milk at an auspicious time.The festival marks the end of winter solstice and the start of the sun's six-month journey northwards.
The festival also includes Bhogi Pongal on the first day, when people burn old household items and perform a puja by cutting paddy, and Maatu Pongal on the third day, when people worship cattle.

3.Mattu Pongal

The third day of the festival, which is dedicated to cattle. People wash and adorn cows with colorful beads, flowers, and bells, and offer them special offerings.

people celebrate Pongal in villages

Offer prayers: People offer prayers to the sun in the open, and at the village temple.
Cook Pongal: People help cook Pongal, learning ancient recipes and stirring the rice.

Wind blowing against the kite's sail is blocked by the sail and is forced to move around the kite.
